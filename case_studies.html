HomeStretch

HomeStretch is an organization that provides economic relief to families in financial distress. The nonporfit initially developed its site as a catch-all solution for every possible thing any user may need.

I joined an agile UX design team as a content strategist and information architecture to redesign the non-profit's website.

Definition

The Results

  • Established the scope of work
  • Drafted proto-personas
  • Defined a value propsition, problem statement, and UX hypothesis

Proto-personas

The team devleoped proto-personas based on exsiting research collected by HomeStreach. The users were broken into three personas: the donor persona, the recipient persona persona, and the volunteer persona.

Rita The Recipient

"I would love to spend more time with my kids in a safe home."

Doug The Donor

"I want to want to give back in a meaningful way."

Valerie The Volunteer

"I want to be the change in my community."

The Donor Persona

Conducting initial user research, the design team uncovered three different personas. After speaking with key stakeholders, the team took the direction to optimize the site for one of the three personas - the donor.

Redlining Navigation

From here, I took responsibility for redlining the site's current navigation. I brought screenshots of the HomeStreach navigation into Figma.

I reviewed the site's functionality and content, making a note of areas that could be improved.

Card Sorting
